Croissant delicacy

A brief history

The flaky, buttery croissant originally comes from Austria, not France. Legend says it celebrated Austria's victory over the Ottoman Turks in 1683, with its shape symbolizing "devouring" the enemy's crescent emblem. The pastry arrived in France in the 1830s when Austrian baker August Zang opened a Viennese bakery in Paris. The French renamed it "croissant" (meaning crescent). The modern, layered butter technique that creates its distinctive flakiness was developed by French bakers in the early 20th century, transforming the once bread-like pastry into the French icon we know today.
